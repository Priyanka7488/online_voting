RASHRTIYA JANTA DAL


The Rashtriya Janta Dal is a centre-left Indian poltical party with political pressure mainly in Bihar. The mass base of the party has traditionally been Yadavs and Muslims, two large and relatively politically active segments of Bihar's population.

President: Lalu Prasad Yadav

ECI status: State party

Founder: Lalu Prasad Yadav, Raghuvansh Prasad Singh, Kanti Singh

Headquarter: 13, V.P. House, Rafi Marg, New Delhi, India

Founded: 1997

Status in Bihar elections: RJD contested as a part of the Mahaghathbandhan (Grand Alliance) in Bihar legislative assembly election 2020. The Mahaghathbandhan including RJD, Congress and Left parties managed to secure 110 seats in the state's 243 seat legislative assembly. The party emerged as the single largest party in the election. In 2008, RJD received the status of recognized national level party following its performance in north-eastern states. RJD was dercognised as a national party on 30 July 2010.
